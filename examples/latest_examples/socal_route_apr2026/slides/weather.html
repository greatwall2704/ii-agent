April Weather Insights — Southern California

Key Takeaways

Late April is typically mild and dry along the coast, with warmer, sunnier conditions inland. Mornings can see a brief marine layer near the ocean; afternoons trend sunny. Evenings are cool — bring layers.

  • Los Angeles (coast): ~69–72°F / 20–22°C highs; ~54–56°F / 12–13°C lows; Apr rain ~0.6 in.
  • San Diego (coast): ~69–71°F / 20–21°C highs; ~57–60°F / 14–16°C lows; Apr rain ~0.65 in.
  • Coastal Malibu & Laguna: similar to LA/SD; breezier near water; ocean ~62–64°F / 17–18°C.
  • Temecula (inland): a few degrees warmer by day; cool nights; very low rain odds.
  • Joshua Tree (desert option): warm, very dry days; strong sun; nights can be cool.
  • Daylight: ~13+ hours; around 06:15 sunrise and ~19:30 sunset (PDT).

Implication: Favor outdoor coastal and vineyard activities midday/afternoon; keep indoor museum options for rare breezy/cool or drizzly hours.

Sources: NOAA-cited climate summaries via Wikipedia (Los Angeles, San Diego) and NPS (Joshua Tree).

Coastal mornings may start cloudy; afternoons trend clear and mild.
